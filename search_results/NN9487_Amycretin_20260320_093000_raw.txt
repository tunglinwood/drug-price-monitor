**Location:** San Antonio, TX, USA
**Enrollment:** 144 participants
**Study Period:** May 11, 2022 - January 9, 2024

## === EFFICACY DATA ===

### Weight Loss Results (Subcutaneous Administration - NCT06064006):

**Part B (60mg once-weekly):**
- **24.3% weight loss** at week 36 (vs -1.1% placebo)
- p<0.0001
- Reference: PMID 40550231

**Part C (20mg once-weekly):**
- **22.0% weight loss** at week 36 (vs 1.9% placebo)
- p<0.0001
- Reference: PMID 40550231

**Part D (5mg once-weekly):**
- **16.2% weight loss** at week 28 (vs 2.3% placebo)
- p<0.0001
- Reference: PMID 40550231

**Part E (1.25mg once-weekly):**
- **9.7% weight loss** at week 20 (vs 2.0% placebo)
- p=0.0003
- Reference: PMID 40550231

### Weight Loss Results (Oral Administration - NCT05369390):

**13.1% weight loss** at 12 weeks (oral formulation)
- Reference: PMID 41850421 (Metabolism 2026 review)

### Preclinical Data (PMID 40706446):

**Diet-Induced Obese Rats (21-day treatment):**
- 47% reduction in total energy intake (95% CI: vehicle 2132-2493 kcal vs amycretin 1044-1390 kcal)
- **18% body weight reduction** (95% CI: vehicle +6.56% to +8.47% vs amycretin -10.48% to -12.74%, p<0.0001)
- Maintained energy expenditure
- Improved insulin sensitivity (higher glucose infusion rates during hyperinsulinaemic-euglycemic clamp)
- Improved MASLD (metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease) histological scores, primarily by reducing steatosis

**Diet-Induced Obese Mice (21-day treatment):**
- Significant reductions in daily and accumulated food intake
- Significant reductions in absolute body weight and body weight change
- Dose-dependent effects at 2 nmol/kg and 10 nmol/kg twice-daily

### Dose-Response Data:

**Subcutaneous (once-weekly):**
- 1.25mg → 9.7% weight loss (week 20)
- 5mg → 16.2% weight loss (week 28)
- 20mg → 22.0% weight loss (week 36)
- 60mg → 24.3% weight loss (week 36)

**Oral (once-daily):**
- 3mg, 6mg, 12mg doses studied in Phase 1
- Up to 50mg once-daily in Part C/D
- 13.1% weight loss at 12 weeks (review data)

### Safety Profile:

**Most Common Adverse Events:**
- Gastrointestinal events (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea)
- Majority mild to moderate in severity
- Resolved by end of study
- 49% of all treatment-emergent adverse events were GI-related
- 81% of participants reporting AEs experienced GI events

**Serious Adverse Events:**
- No deaths reported
- High proportion of discontinuations occurred due to reasons unrelated to treatment
